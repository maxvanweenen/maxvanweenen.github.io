Max van Weenen

I’m an ambitious Dutch applied psychologist and people person at heart. At a young age I developed a fascination for human behavior. The main questions I had where: What drives people? What process leads to certain behaviour? and mostly: How can you affect it? During my study in Applied Psychology I realized there where no short and easy answers for these questions, but they gave me the tools to analyse and guide behaviour in a wide context to achieve desirable goals on an personal or organisational level.

Now that I finished my study, I realize that this doesn’t mean I learned everything about behaviour, and I am truly enthusiastic about developing my skills and learn more in an organisation which I can grow alongside of.

Portfolio

Research & advice: Room for talent

Anonymized publication
A study for a global media and marketing services company. The goal was to find ways for employees to use their talents, skills and interests within the organization.

The reason for the study was that the employees had directly expressed the need to make more use of what they and their co-workers had to offer next to the skills they put to use in their current roles.

Room for talent proposed a variety of recommendations and an implementation plan to fulfill the needs and achieve the research objective.

Knowledge inventory

A study to map out the knowledge about work related laws among about 270 employees.

The study gave clear insight in what knowledge was lacking in certain departments. By using this insight all that was needed was some short targeted trainings to get an acceptable knowledge level in all departments.

Assessments: Talentscan

Anonymized assessment summary
Assessments are psychological tests used to learn either general or specific facts about another person, either to inform others of how they function now, or to predict their behaviour and functioning in the future.

I've obtained a good amount of practice with all kinds of assessments and assessment tools over the last few years. An example is the Talentscan.

The Talentscan:
  • Determined what jobs made best use of the clients talents.
  • Proved a list of jobs the client was likely to enjoy.
  • Gave insight in what kind of organisations the client would enjoy working in.

Training: Track the talent

A two hour training developed for the organisation: Track the talent. This organisation offers youth a program in which they participate in weekly training to increase their personal and social developments and increase their chances on the job market.

This particular training aimed to teach the participants to recognize their own talents and find ways to develop them further.

Training: Inzicht in veiligheid

A three hour long training about the basics of changing behaviour. The skills obtained in the training would be used to stimulate a safe work environment at dangerous work places.

During the training the participants:
  • Actively Practised with psychological tools like ASE and the four force model.
  • Used personal example cases to relate to the information.
  • Developed insight on triggers that lead to safe work behaviour.
